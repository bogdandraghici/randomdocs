Un-bundling Banking & Logistics.

Three ways to separate the two audiences on the homepage — same URL, no split into separate sites. Scheme for discussion; nothing is implemented.

1 · The problem

The homepage promises three industries, then serves one

6 / 7value streams in the hero are financial services
4 / 4featured agent stacks read banking / insurance
0 / 6testimonials come from logistics
→ /bankingwhere the "Explore Industries" button sends everyone

It's not 50/50 bundling — it's a financial-services page with logistics stapled to the headline. A logistics buyer is promised in the hero, then abandoned for the rest of the scroll. A banking buyer trips over freight vocabulary in their first screen.

Prerequisite for every direction: logistics needs homepage-grade proof — at least one testimonial and one featured agent stack. The raw material exists (140-agent catalog, full /logistics page).

Direction B

One URL, three homepages

A toggle in the hero swaps each section's content per industry — copy, stacks, testimonials. Remembered per visitor, presettable via ?industry= for ads and nav links.

Direction C

One page, two chapters

Shared neutral top, then the scroll splits into clearly labeled industry chapters — each with its own stacks, proof, and CTA. Everyone sees everything, but nothing masquerades as "for you".

3 · The call

Recommendation

A now — C later if the homepage should carry industry proof itself. B only if marketing needs per-industry campaign behavior on the homepage specifically; pointing campaigns at the existing industry pages gets most of that for free.
