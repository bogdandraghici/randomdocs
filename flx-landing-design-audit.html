01 · Verdict

Not AI slop. An art-directed system.

Two independent assessments, one conclusion: the visual layer is Linear/Palantir-tier; the content layer is what undermines it.

Overall impression

A designer would say "someone with taste built this." The single-amber discipline holds, composition varies genuinely from page to page, the stats are drawn as instruments rather than hero-metric templates, and the deterministic scanner found effectively zero real anti-patterns across 90+ files. The redesign's identity, a terminal-and-instrument world where the scrollbar feels like a flight control, is distinctive and worth protecting.

What lets it down is trust arithmetic: the hero claims "Over 220 agents" while the same viewport stamps "140+", a heading promises twelve artifact kinds over nine cards, and the ROI flow says "email me" then opens a print dialog and posts the lead to a public Google Form. For a brand selling auditable AI to banks, the content contradictions are the one thing a skeptical buyer needs to dismiss everything else. They are also the cheapest things on this list to fix.

03 · What works

Protect these at all costs.

Six things done genuinely well, and why they work. Any future iteration should treat these as load-bearing.

W1The hero scroll narrative

Overview → value streams → governance → terminal is a story, not a slideshow. The persistent mono HUD (AZ · EL · R · SCROLL) makes the scrollbar feel like a flight instrument. This is the site's signature move and nobody else in the category has it.

W2The agents registry as a table

stack / agents / covers / status in mono, with "your-stack — describe it in the compiler above" as the last row. A sales pitch disguised as infrastructure. The best single section on the site.

W3Proof numbers drawn as instruments

Every stat ships a bespoke SVG meter: an 80% proportion rail, a $1.8M ruler with ten $0.2M gradations, week 8 marked on a 12-week quarter. SSR renders final values for crawlers; JS counts up only when motion is allowed. Exemplary.

W4The ROI mad-lib sentence

Six parameters as one editable sentence instead of a form, an honest break-even chart that models re-investment dips, and a full "no black box" methodology section below it. The emotional high point of the site for a buyer.

W5Domain-authentic micro-fictions

Audit rows ("chain verified · block 84k"), the banking decision-log hero streaming live underwriting events, agent cards with counters ("deviations flagged 2 · playbook match 96%"). These sell "in production" better than testimonials.

W6Motion and accent discipline

One global reduced-motion kill switch, hover strictly limited to actionable elements, a single amber accent that survives ~5,200 lines of CSS, and amber ::selection so even text selection is on-brand.

04 · Priority issues

Ten issues, ranked.

P0 blocks trust or users outright; P1 is major; P2 moderate; P3 polish. Every claim below was verified against source or live captures.

P0The agent count contradicts itself inside one viewport
What
Hero sub-copy says "Over 220 enterprise-ready AI agents" while the HUD stamp on the same screen says "140+ enterprise-ready agents" (app/page.jsx:74 vs :54). The registry lede repeats 220 while its own table footer says 140+ (:239 vs :277). One blog post contains both "more than 220" and a "140+" callout three lines apart. Ground truth: lib/agentsData.js contains exactly 140 agents, and "20 categories" matches nothing in the data (3 industries · 34 sectors · 119 stacks).
Why
This site sells auditable numbers to regulated buyers. A bank CTO who catches marketing math that doesn't reconcile has a permanent reason to distrust the product math, including the −40% cost stat they came for.
Fix
Export one constant from agentsData.js (e.g. AGENT_COUNT = AGENTS.length) and interpolate it everywhere an agent count appears: hero, stamp, nav, registry, page metadata, industry pages. Delete "220" and "20 categories" or make them true in the data first.
P0No 404 or error page exists
What
app/not-found.jsx and app/error.jsx don't exist. A mistyped URL or dead campaign link lands on Next.js's unstyled white default, on a site this meticulously art-directed.
Why
Total brand collapse at the exact moment a visitor is already lost. The site has many cross-links and redirects (/hub, /agents → /banking, model slugs) that make dead ends reachable.
Fix
Ship the terminal 404 described in Delight #1 below. It is one static component, and it turns the site's biggest hole into its most quotable easter egg.
P1"Email me this estimate" doesn't email anything, and the lead goes to a Google Form
What
The ROI calculator's highest-intent button collects name, email and consent, then opens a browser print dialog (RoiCalculator.jsx:447, w.print()). The modal submit even says "Submit & download PDF". Meanwhile the lead POSTs to a public docs.google.com/forms endpoint (:42).
Why
A broken promise at maximum trust. And a vendor whose home page says "Impeccable Data Privacy · zero data egress by default" sending PII to Google Forms is a dissonance any technical evaluator will spot in DevTools in five seconds.
Fix
Rename the CTA to what it does ("Download the summary (PDF)") or make it do what it says (server-side email). Route the lead into the same HubSpot portal the demo modal already uses, one CRM, one consent text.
P1"Twelve kinds of platform artifact" sits above nine cards
What
app/flowx-code/page.jsx:127 promises twelve; the grid lists nine (Integration → Data model); the stat block at :193 re-asserts "12".
Why
The flowx-code page targets developers, the persona most likely to count. One wrong number on a page about spec-driven correctness undercuts the page's entire thesis.
Fix
Add the three missing kinds, or retitle to "Nine kinds" / add a "9 of 12 shown — full list in docs" footnote.
P1The cookie pill sits on top of content on mobile
What
The fixed bottom-left consent pill overlaps ledes and the ROI sentence's investment field at 390px until dismissed (seen in home-mobile and roi-mobile captures).
Why
It occludes real UI, including an input, on the device class where every pixel is contested.
Fix
On mobile, render it as a full-width bottom bar that pushes content, or collapse to a small icon after the first scroll.
P1The docs promise a light theme the site no longer has
What
layout.jsx:15-19 pins data-theme="dark" ("no toggle, no light theme"), yet CLAUDE.md and README still advertise a neutral-paper light theme that "ships alongside" dark, globals.css carries the full light token contract as dead weight, and .nav__theme remains as an invisible, non-functional 34px button in the nav.
Why
A false claim in the design contract plus dead UI is a maintenance trap: the next contributor will either resurrect a stale theme or waste time discovering it's disabled. (This audit lost 20 minutes to exactly that.)
Fix
Decide. Either ship the light theme properly (it's 90% built), or delete the scaffolding, the invisible button, and the doc claims in one PR.
P2Five names for two actions
What
On the home page alone: "Book a demo" (nav), "Explore Agents" + "Calculate ROI" (hero), "Explore Industries" + "Calculate ROI" (registry), "Book a build review" (blueprint), "Schedule a customized demo" + "Explore Agents" (final CTA). Most resolve to the same #demo modal.
Why
Each new label makes the visitor re-decide whether this is the same action. Conversion asks should be boring and identical.
Fix
One primary verb phrase site-wide ("Book a demo"). Context-specific labels only where the destination genuinely differs.
P2Hero governance labels collide
What
In hero step 3, the instrument labels "OBSERVABILITY" and "PII" overlap into an unreadable blur, and a left-side label clips behind the headline (visible in the step-3 capture).
Why
The HUD layer is the brand's precision statement; the one place it garbles text is the step about governance, the worst possible place to look imprecise.
Fix
Simple label-repulsion or a collision fade in slowTide.js's callout placement.
P3The platform page drifts into generic card grids
What
/platform's "What's inside" is six identical eyebrow-title-text cards; "Why it holds up" is a 3+1 grid with an orphan card on its own row.
Why
It's the one page that looks like every other SaaS site, sitting in the middle of a site that otherwise never does. The orphan row also breaks the grid's own logic.
Fix
Fold "Why it holds up" into the mono spec-table idiom the registry already owns; vary the "What's inside" cells by content type the way the home platform grid does (audit log, rails, router).
P3Same stat, two owners
What
"80% of manual handoffs automated" is attributed to "European commercial bank" on home (page.jsx:417) and to "COO · Major Custodian Bank" on /customers (customersData.js:52).
Why
Anonymized case studies survive scrutiny only if the anonymization is internally consistent.
Fix
Single source stats with their attribution in customersData.js and render both surfaces from it.
05 · Inconsistencies

The full drift list.

Everything found across both assessments, grouped. The visual system barely drifts; nearly all of this is content and interaction plumbing.

Copy & data

  • 01140+ vs "Over 220" agents: 140+ in layout metadata, hero stamp, hero step 2, registry footer, nav (×2), platform, banking, insurance, logistics, ai-agents; 220 in hero sub, registry lede, and the FlowX 6 blog post (which uses both). Data says exactly 140.
  • 02"20 categories" appears three times on home; no 20-way categorization exists anywhere in agentsData.js.
  • 03"Twelve kinds" of platform artifact over nine cards on /flowx-code; the stat block repeats "12".
  • 04The 80% handoffs stat has two different anonymized owners on home vs /customers.
  • 05Origin story told three ways: "2013 founded" (about hero), "2021 — a new beginning, we launched FlowX.AI" (about timeline), "For 10+ years we've shipped AI" (home). Possibly all true; reads as three different companies.
  • 06"compile ↑" in the registry links to #blueprint, which is below it (the Agent Builder section), while the row's copy says the compiler is above (the hero terminal). Wrong direction, wrong target, or wrong copy: pick one.

Pattern & component drift

  • 07Identical CTA slots with different destination logic: hero primary goes to /ai-agents, registry primary to /banking.
  • 08Two accordion idioms: FAQ uses native <details> with a "+" marker; the ROI methodology uses a custom disclosure pattern.
  • 09Section rail labels are usually nouns ("Agents", "Proof", "Voices") but occasionally full phrases ("What it assumes — and what it means"), which breaks their scanning rhythm.
  • 10/platform's "Why it holds up" leaves an orphan card in a 3-column grid; every other grid on the site resolves evenly.

Color & accent

  • 11The ROI print report uses ambers #B8860B and #8a5e00; neither is the brand's --amber #FCB813 or a token.
  • 12The blog index shows ten amber tag pills at once, the densest accent screen on the site, diluting the "resolved outcome" meaning amber carries in the nav's featured cards.

Theme & interaction

  • 13Light theme promised in docs, fully tokenized in CSS, disabled in code; invisible dead toggle button still in the nav; themechange listeners in every canvas module can never fire.
  • 14Two lead pipelines: demo modal → HubSpot; ROI report → public Google Form. Two CRMs, two consent texts, one company.

Mobile vs desktop

  • 15Cookie pill overlaps content (including a form field) at 390px; desktop clearance is explicitly handled, mobile isn't.
  • 16The hero HUD telemetry line wraps into the "scroll" indicator on mobile.
06 · Delight

Delight is already the strategy. Compound it.

The site's delight doesn't need confetti; it needs more moments where the terminal-and-instrument world pays attention back. First, what already sparks:

  • HUD telemetry tied to real scroll: AZ · EL · R · SCROLL updating as the camera descends.
  • The compile terminal with hint chips and a streaming blueprint.yaml ("writing…" → spec).
  • "your-stack · compile ↑": a CTA wearing a table row's clothes.
  • Proof-meter rulers with true gradations ($0.2M ticks, fortnight ticks).
  • Two-office clocks with coordinates and live local times on /about.
  • Break-even notch chart that models yearly re-investment dips instead of a smooth hockey stick.
  • Amber text selection: even ::selection is on-brand.
  • Banking decision-log hero streaming live underwriting events with a LIVE dot.

07 · Personas

Three walk-throughs, three failure sets.

What actually breaks for the people this site must convince.

The skeptical bank CTO · vendor evaluation

  • Catches 140 vs 220 within one scroll, then re-reads every other number with suspicion, including the −40% cost stat they came for.
  • Inspects the ROI form in DevTools and finds PII flowing to docs.google.com/forms, from a vendor whose home page says "zero data egress by default".
  • Finds no single named, dated case study anywhere; anonymized quotes plus the honest disclaimer help, but there's nothing referenceable to take to a board.
  • Responds well to: the audit-log fiction, the methodology section, the governance vocabulary. The raw material for convincing this persona is all here.

The first-time visitor · from a LinkedIn ad

  • Must scroll ~4 pinned viewports of (beautiful) hero before the "95% of AI initiatives fail" hook, the actual pitch.
  • Meets five differently-worded CTAs and can't tell which one is "the" intended action.
  • Hits jargon ("value streams", "agent stacks", "ontology layer") before any of it is defined.
  • On mobile, reads around a cookie pill parked over the content.

The developer · evaluating agent-builder and flowx-code

  • Counts things. Finds "Twelve kinds" over nine cards on the page most explicitly aimed at people who count.
  • Wants a runnable artifact and gets none: no sandbox, no curl, no schema link; "See how it works" scrolls to marketing sections.
  • Gets no keyboard affordances, no changelog surface, no API teaser on-site.
  • Responds well to: the best writing on the site lives here ("A workflow can be deterministic even when its nodes are not."). The voice is right; the artifacts are missing.

09 · Questions

Five questions worth arguing about.

  1. What is the one number FlowX defends under audit?The site currently offers 140, 220, 20, 12, 9, 95%, 80%. If a regulator-minded buyer can't reconcile the marketing math, why would they trust the agent math? Pick one canonical dataset and render every claim from it.
  2. Does the hero earn its four viewports?The sequence is beautiful, but it front-loads the peak and taxes the skeptic. What if governance (step 3) arrived after the "95% fail" section, as the answer instead of the overture?
  3. If the terminal is the brand, why does conversion abandon it?You compile a blueprint in a terminal; then the CTA is a HubSpot modal. What would booking a demo look like as a compile target: $ schedule --with your-stack?
  4. Is amber allowed to mean more than one thing?In the nav's featured cards it marks "the resolved outcome". Blog tags, FAQ markers and heading periods also spend it. Define amber's single semantic and enforce it like a token, not a color.
  5. Who is the second visit for?Everything is optimized for the first scroll. A returning evaluator finds no changelog, no live status, no named case study, no way to resume their compiled blueprint. What persists for them?
